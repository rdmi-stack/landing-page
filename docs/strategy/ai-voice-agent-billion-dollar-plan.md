Generic **web / app / software development** search demand is declining (commodity AI is eating it) and was never the path to a billion-dollar agency anyway — it's high-volume, ₹30-CPC, race-to-the-bottom work. The data points to a single, cleaner opportunity: **AI Receptionist / AI Voice Agents** — global 9,900 searches/mo, **+61% growth, rising in 18 of 18 countries checked**, with HIGH US advertiser competition (proof the money is real). ## 1. The Problem We're Solving

**Observation that started this:** "web design / software / app development search trends are declining over recent years."

**Verified (Google Ads Keyword Planner API, India, trailing 12 mo):**

| Term | India vol/mo | Drift (12 mo) |
|---|---|---|
| web development | 90,500 | noisy, softening |
| web development company | 12,100 | ↓ ~45% |
| software development company | 8,100 | ↓ ~45% |
| app development company | 5,400 | ↓ ~33% |
| mobile app development company | 8,100 | ~flat |

**The real insight isn't the decline — it's that high search volume on these terms is a trap:**
- "web development" = 90,500/mo but **₹30 CPC in India** → commodity, zero margin, knife-fight.
- A billion-dollar agency is **never** built on generic-dev search clicks. (Accenture, Deloitte Digital, EPAM, Thoughtworks didn't get to $1B via Google Ads volume — they did it on enterprise relationships, retainers, and outcomes.)
- **AI is compressing the value of generic dev** (anyone can scaffold a site/app), so the declining search is a *symptom of commoditization*, not a market we want to defend.

**Conclusion:** Move up the stack — from "we build software" (commodity) to "we deploy AI that runs your operations" (outcome, recurring, defensible).

## 2. What Actually Builds a $1B Agency (the selection criteria)

Search volume is the **wrong** metric. The right criteria:

| Criterion | Why | Generic dev | AI voice agent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Deal size** | $1B needs big deals, not many tiny ones | $5–50K | $50–500K+ / recurring |
| **Recurring revenue** | One-off projects can't compound to $1B | build-and-leave | monthly retainer / per-seat / per-minute |
| **Defensibility** | commodity = margin death | anyone codes a site | domain + vertical expertise compounds |
| **Budget trajectory** | ride an expanding budget | AI is *eating* it | AI is *expanding* ops budgets |
| **Outcome-priced** | sell results, not hours | "₹X for a website" | "never miss a call, cut no-shows 40%" |

**Cross-check against RDMI's own growth thesis (memory):** the constraint is *close rate*, not lead-gen. So worrying about category search *volume* is the wrong fear — deal *quality*, positioning, and recurring revenue matter more. This plan optimizes for those.

## 7. Honest Caveats (so confidence rests on solid ground)

1. **12-month cap.** The Keyword Planner API returns only the trailing ~12 months — this is *recent-year* growth, **not** a multi-year trend. For the 3-year question, **Google Trends** is the only free source (but it's relative-interest 0–100, not absolute volume).
2. **Recency under-reporting.** The latest 1–2 months read artificially low and get revised up later. Always interpret growth **relative to the basket**, never as a precise %.
3. **Bucketing.** Planner snaps volumes to fixed tiers (e.g. 4,400 / 5,400 / 6,600 / 8,100 / 9,900 / 12,100). One tier-step looks like a big % move but can be noise.
4. **CPC currency.** All CPCs are in the **ad account's currency (INR)** regardless of geo — a *relative* cross-country signal, not the live local USD/AED rate. (Pattern observed: the West pays 10–40× the developing world per click.)
5. **Small-volume noise.** Terms under ~200/mo (most vertical terms today) swing wildly on a handful of searches. Their +300% growth signals *budget forming*, but they're **outbound/relationship markets, not search markets yet.**
6. **Competition is data too.** HIGH competition = validated money but harder/pricier entry; LOW = earlier but unproven. The vertical wedge is how we get validated-money economics at low-competition CPCs.
